Southern Journey: Everyday Life from the Edge of the Galaxy

A Tintin-style illustrated travel comic

The Blue House at Dusk

In a corner of the galaxy, a blue house with its lights on stands quietly. Perhaps a cosmic post office? "Has the message from afar already crossed light-years to reach us?"

Gazing at the Lakeside Town

With a camera on his shoulder, he watches the ripples and distant rooftops, like a page from an unfinished adventure. "Scenery is also a form of story."

The Ice Cream Mystery

A curious little sign by the roadside: 'Pudú Artisan Ice Cream'. Who or what is Pudú? And what exactly is melting? "If I'm not in an ice cream shop, I'm probably on my way to one."

The Volcano and the 'X'

A snowy volcano stands silent like a deity. The 'X' ahead seems like a cosmic clue: 'Something is hidden here.' "Sometimes the direction is revealed in the unexpected."

A Stroll Uphill

Her silhouette fades into the road ahead, while the church spires in the distance seem to watch over her. "Every step is a panel in your own comic book."

Sailing into Dusk

The lake sparkles under the setting sun as a lone sailboat drifts toward the mountains. "Some say romance is fiction—I see it in sails and snowy peaks."
